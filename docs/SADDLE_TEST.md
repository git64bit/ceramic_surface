## Full saddle checks

1. The TPU saddle seats fully against the ceramic edge.
2. The three grip ribs contact the opposing ceramic face.
3. The corner-relief grooves prevent concentrated pressure on both ceramic edge radii.
4. The PLA+ insert slides into the TPU guides without tools.
5. The insert remains captured when the tile is lifted and turned.
6. The insert reaches the far stop.
7. The PLA+ contact boss touches the ceramic before the surrounding TPU datum jaw carries load.
8. The external PLA+ deck does not rock relative to the ceramic.
9. The saddle can be removed with a straight pull and no sideways prying.

## Simple handling test

With no wedge attached, install the saddle and datum insert, then lift and carry the tile while supporting it normally. The saddle should remain in place under its own grip. Do not suspend the entire tile only from the saddle during this initial test.

## Load-path test

Place the tile on a stable surface with the saddle datum deck facing downward. Apply hand pressure directly over the saddle. Record whether:

- the PLA+ deck remains firm;
- the TPU visibly compresses in the height direction;
- the insert shifts in its guide rails;
- the saddle creeps away from the ceramic edge.

The correct fit offset and any required insert-clearance adjustment should be settled before the wedge-to-saddle adapter is designed.
